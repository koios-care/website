The gap

Two visits a year. Eight thousand hours of life.

~0.6%of a year with Parkinson’s is spent in front of a neurologist. The rest is recalled from memory at the next appointment.

Neurology runs on snapshots: a short exam, a questionnaire, and a patient trying to recall how a hard month felt. But symptoms shift hour to hour, and quality of life is difficult to capture in a fifteen-minute appointment.

So medication gets adjusted slowly, decline shows up late, and no one can measure whether care is working. Koios covers that gap — turning the time between visits into objective data that patients and clinicians can act on.

How Koios works

Everyday devices. Clinically meaningful signal.

No clinic-grade hardware. No study wearable to charge. No daily diary to fill in. Just the phone and watch people already own.

1

Wear what you already wear

A smartphone and a smartwatch. Parkiwatch runs quietly in the background — no tasks to complete, no change to daily routine.

2

Turn movement into measures

Gait, tremor, sleep, activity and heart-rate physiology become 40+ validated digital measures of neuro, brain and body health.

3

Track quality of life with the Q-index

One clear index shows how quality of life changes over time. Patients can share it with their care team to help guide treatment.

Koios Care is a wellness solution. It is not a medical device and does not diagnose or treat disease.
